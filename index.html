01 / Our story

More than a haircut

Care, confidence & a chair for everyone.

At just 16, Ava Zhang is Virginia’s youngest licensed cosmetologist and the founder of Cuts for a Cause, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit reimagining beauty as a source of dignity, agency, and belonging.

Ava leads haircutting sessions and cosmetology lessons at shelters throughout the DMV—DC, Maryland, and Virginia—often working for up to seven hours at a time. By serving every age and hair type, she has built partnerships with close to every shelter in Virginia and empowered more than 850 Virginians to date.

Her work is grounded in a simple belief: beauty care can create space for healing, self-expression, and genuine human connection.

Each haircut carries a story

Returning care builds lasting trust.

Through repeated visits, Ava has formed deep connections with clients and created a chair where people can feel heard as well as cared for. A haircut is never treated as a one-time service; it is an opportunity to restore confidence and help someone reclaim how they move through the world.

George · Fauquier Family Shelter

“Coming back to Virginia, I found this shelter. I love having volunteers like you come in and help. It really does make me feel seen and valued. I finally feel like I have some support, you know?”

After losing his leg to an infection while pursuing a music career in Texas, George returned to Virginia without adequate support or funds. His story reflects the value of consistent care and being recognized as a whole person.

A new beginning · Fair Ridge Shelter

“I’ve always struggled with my body image and looks, but this new hair makes me feel more confident than I have ever been.”

After sharing that mistreatment tied to her sexuality and appearance had made it difficult to embrace her identity, a young Latina client asked Ava for a fresh start. Ava shaped a sleek bob around her features, helping her feel prepared and confident for upcoming job interviews.

The circumstances facing many clients are structural. Limited access to resources reinforces cycles of marginalization: people are judged for their appearance while lacking the means to change it. Cuts for a Cause meets immediate grooming needs while challenging the deeper stigma surrounding poverty.
